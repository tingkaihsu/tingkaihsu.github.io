Provisional Research Note for On-shell Approach to Anomalous Thresholds

Ting-Kai Hsu
2025-12-12
research_project
others, research_note

This document is a provisional research note summarizing the current status of an ongoing project. I began my theoretical-physics research with Professor Yu-tin Huang at National Taiwan University (NTU), developing an on-shell approach to anomalous thresholds in subtracted dispersion relations for S-matrices with unstable external states. Anomalous thresholds invalidate positivity bounds in effective field theories (EFTs) by introducing double discontinuities that break the unitarity-based derivation. With unknown ultraviolet (UV) states in loops, explicit branch-cut analysis becomes intractable, making the necessary subtractions impossible within EFTs. To address this obstacle, I worked with established EFT models and known UV spectra to develop a practical solution. Through the method-of-regions calculations, I identified that anomalous-threshold effects arise solely from the soft region, where the loop momentum is parametrically smaller than heavy UV masses. I encountered another obstacle when I tried to extend this finding to the on-shell approach. Initially, I assumed that generalized unitarity cuts would straightforwardly apply within EFTs; however, explicit loop calculations revealed subtleties when integrating out UV propagators. Through sustained computational work, I was able to express the anomalous-threshold effects in terms of infrared observables by carefully analyzing dispersive sum rules, which reorganize unknown UV information into Wilson coefficients. This experience sharpened my grasp of modern amplitude techniques and taught me an essential lesson: recognizing what I do not yet understand is as crucial as applying what I know.
